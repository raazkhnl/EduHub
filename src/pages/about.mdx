# About

**EduHub** is a free, open-source academic portal for Nepali engineering students.
It exists because every batch of students rebuilds the same drive of PDFs from scratch,
loses it three years later, and never publishes their version. This site is one attempt
to make that work cumulative instead of disposable.

## What's here

- **Chapter notes** for select subjects across IOE, CTEVT, and TU curricula —
  written or curated by the maintainers and contributors, in prose that prioritises
  understanding over completeness of bullet points.
- **Past papers** with year tags and (where licensed) suggested solutions.
- **Lab manuals**, **slide decks**, and **reference reading**, all under a single
  taxonomy so you don't have to guess where things live.

## What's not here

- We don't host pirated copies of copyrighted textbooks. If a reference belongs in
  a chapter we link to the legitimate source.
- We don't claim to be official. Curricula on this site reflect the publicly
  available syllabi as of their last update — check the **Last updated** stamp at
  the bottom of every page, and verify against your institution's authoritative
  copy before treating anything as definitive.

## Who maintains it

Started and maintained by [RaaZ Khanal](https://khanalrajesh.com.np), a computer
engineer based in Nepal. Contributors are credited per file via the Git history,
which the "Edit on GitHub" link on every page lets you join.

## How to help

- **Read a page and spot a mistake?** Use the "Edit this page" link at the bottom
  — it opens the source file directly in GitHub's editor.
- **Want to add a subject?** Read the [contributing guide](/contribute) and the
  taxonomy reference. Most subjects take one well-structured PR.
- **Can't write but can share?** Send the link to a junior. Network effects matter
  more than commits.
